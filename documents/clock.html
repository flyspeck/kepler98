The Countdown Clock

This clock counts down to the date of expected completion.

It was set in motion on October 12, 1997. On that day, the remaining parts of the Kepler conjecture were divided up into 27,000 credits. A rough guess was made of the difficulty of each step, and the credits were divided accordingly.

The project completion date is computed by the formula 

  completionDate = startingDate + 
	(earnedCredits/totalCredits)*
		(currentDate-startingDate)
This will underestimate the actual completion date because the easiest tasks will tend to be completed first.

  • A ledger gives technical details about how the credits were divided.
